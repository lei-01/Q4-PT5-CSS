Protecting the Marikina River

The Marikina River is one of the city’s most vital natural resources, yet it faces persistent threats from pollution, illegal dumping, and unregulated development along its banks. I believe that protecting the river should be a top priority for both the city government and residents, because a clean and well-maintained river reduces flood risks, supports local biodiversity, and enhances the quality of life for everyone in Marikina.

One reason for this urgency is the recurring flooding during heavy rains, which often affects nearby communities due to siltation and blocked waterways. Additionally, the accumulation of garbage and industrial waste not only harms aquatic life but also impacts public health. To address these issues, the city can implement stricter environmental regulations, conduct regular river clean-up drives, and promote community education programs that encourage residents to dispose of waste responsibly. Public-private partnerships with local businesses and schools can further amplify these initiatives.

I urge every Marikina resident to take an active role in river protection, whether by participating in clean-up drives, planting trees along riverbanks, or simply avoiding littering. Protecting the Marikina River is not just an environmental responsibility—it is a commitment to the safety, health, and future of our community. By acting together, we can ensure that the river remains a source of life, beauty, and pride for generations to come.
