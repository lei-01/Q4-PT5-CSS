SDO-MARIKINA IN RSPC 2026

Sdo-Marikina Makes History, Secures Top 3 Spot In Rspc 2026

Las Piñas City – In a monumental achievement for the Schools Division Office (SDO) of Marikina, its campus journalists clinched a historic Top 3 finish at the Regional Schools Press Conference held on March 14, 2026, at the University of Perpetual Help System DALTA in Las Piñas City. The milestone victory secures SDO-Marikina’s spot in the upcoming National Schools Press Conference to be held in Ormoc City this April. The remarkable performance highlights the exceptional talent and dedication of Marikina’s young journalists. “This is a testament to the hard work and passion of our students and their coaches,” said Janet Cajuguiran, Education Program Supervisor in English for SDO-Marikina, adding that the students have proven their capabilities on the regional stage.

The RSPC is an annual competition organized by Department of Education that gathers the best student writers, broadcasters, and artists from across the National Capital Region to showcase their journalistic skills. SDO-Marikina’s unprecedented success this year not only brings pride to the division but also sets a new benchmark for future campus journalists and inspires more students to pursue journalism. Meanwhile, Schools Division Superintendent Alejandro G. Ibañez and Assistant Schools Division Superintendent Jennifer F. Vivas expressed their gratitude to the Marikina City Government, led by Marjorie Ann A. Teodoro, for their continuous and valuable support to the journalism program of SDO-Marikina.

MARIKINA IMPLEMENTS 4-DAY WORK WEEK FOR ENERGY CONSERVATION

City Government Introduces Alternative Work Schedule for Sustainability

In line with the directive of Ferdinand Marcos Jr. to promote energy conservation, Marjorie Ann A. Teodoro signed Executive Order No. 5 implementing a four-day work week in the Marikina City Government. Under the new policy, government offices will operate from Monday to Thursday. Office hours will also be extended from 8:00 AM to 7:00 PM to ensure that services remain available to the public. The initiative aims to reduce energy consumption in government facilities and support national energy-saving efforts. At the same time, the city government assures residents that productivity and efficiency will be maintained.

Despite the adjusted schedule, the city government emphasized that essential offices will continue to operate. Field operatives and emergency personnel will remain on duty to ensure that important services are not interrupted. This measure guarantees that the needs of residents in Marikina City will still be met. The policy is also expected to help lower electricity use and operational costs in government offices. Through this initiative, the Marikina City Government hopes to encourage responsible energy use while maintaining quality public service.

DPWH INSPECTS FLOOD CONTROL PROJECTS IN MARIKINA

Ongoing river improvements aim to strengthen drainage and reduce flooding risks

Vince Dizon, Secretary of the Department of Public Works and Highways, joined officials of the Marikina City Government and city engineers in inspecting ongoing flood control projects along the Marikina River on Wednesday, March 4. The inspection aimed to evaluate the progress of infrastructure projects designed to protect nearby communities from flooding. Officials reviewed several developments focused on improving drainage systems in flood-prone areas. These projects are part of broader efforts to strengthen disaster preparedness within Marikina City. The visit also allowed authorities to identify areas that may require further improvements.

During the inspection, engineers presented updates on riverbank protection and other flood mitigation measures. Authorities emphasized the importance of maintaining strong flood control systems, especially during the rainy season. The projects are expected to help manage rising water levels and reduce the risk of severe flooding. Local officials highlighted that these improvements will benefit residents living near the river and other vulnerable communities. Once completed, the projects aim to provide safer and more resilient infrastructure for the people of Marikina.

MARIKINA HONORS NEW NURSES FROM PLMar

City recognizes board passers and a national Top 7 in the February 2026 Nurses Licensure Examination

The Marikina City Government, led by Marjorie Ann A. Teodoro, honored graduates of Pamantasan ng Lungsod ng Marikina who successfully passed the February 2026 Nurses Licensure Examination. The recognition ceremony celebrated the graduates’ dedication, perseverance, and commitment to the healthcare profession. City officials commended the new nurses for their hard work throughout their academic journey. The event also served as a moment of pride for the university and the local community. Many attendees praised the graduates for bringing honor to Marikina City.

Among those recognized was Angel Joyce Robles Lopez, who achieved Top 7 in the national examination. Her accomplishment brought pride and recognition to both the university and the city. Mayor Teodoro highlighted that the achievement reflects the quality education provided by the institution. She also emphasized the determination and discipline shown by the students in reaching their goals. The city government expressed hope that the newly licensed nurses will serve communities with compassion and professionalism as they begin their careers in healthcare.

MARIKINA MARKS WORLD KIDNEY DAY 2026

City promotes awareness and preventive care for better kidney health

Marjorie Ann A. Teodoro joined residents and health advocates in commemorating World Kidney Day in Marikina City. The event highlighted the importance of protecting kidney health and promoting preventive healthcare among the public. This year’s observance carried the theme “Kidney Health for All: Caring for People, Protecting the Planet.” The theme encourages communities to become more aware of kidney-related diseases and their impact on people’s lives. It also calls for stronger actions to protect both human health and the environment.

In her message, Mayor Teodoro emphasized that Marikina’s culture of care begins with individuals taking responsibility for their own health. She reminded residents that kidney disease can affect people of any age. According to health advocates, the illness is often detected only when it has already progressed. To help prevent kidney-related diseases, the mayor encouraged the public to maintain a healthy lifestyle. She urged residents to eat balanced meals, drink enough water, exercise regularly, and undergo routine medical checkups.
